Capiz Provincial Park
Capiz Provincial Park is a tranquil landmark nestled in the heart of Roxas City, Philippines. It’s a place where nature and history intertwine, featuring verdant landscapes, pocket gardens, and a charming lagoon with a rocky fountain centerpiece. This park serves as a peaceful sanctuary for those seeking to unwind amidst the beauty of native trees and plants endemic to the Philippines.

Culajao Mangrove
Adventure awaits at Culajao Mangrove Eco Park, where you can learn more about mangroves. It’s an eco-park filled with about 19 mangrove species, 11 bird species, and a great nature spot. It’s a good place to discover ecotourism through its educational tours. They have cottages here where you can go for a picnic. There are other activities you can do here like fishing, too!

What's special in Roxas?

THE SEAFOOD CAPITAL OF THE PHILIPPINES


Discover the charm of Roxas City, the capital of Capiz, a destination that offers a delightful blend of culture, history, and natural wonders. From the cultural and historical artifacts at the Roxas City Museum to the serene river cruise at Palina Greenbelt Ecopark, there's something for everyone. Stroll along the riverfront promenade at Paseo del Rio, visit the historic Casa de Mercado, or relax at the picturesque Roxas City Plaza. Indulge in fresh seafood at the Seafood Court of People's Park while enjoying the beachfront view at Baybay Beach, as Roxas City is known as the "Seafood Capital of the Philippines".

THE MAYOR OF ROXAS CITY

Ronnie Dadivas


Mayor Ronnie, known among his constituents in Roxas City, values integrity, pro-activity, faith, and community in his service to the people. With his Signature line "“…we should always remember why we are here and for whom we work for. Always our matra should be – ‘Tanan para sa kaayuhan sang Roxas City kag sang pumuloyo”.


His first term as the Mayor of Roxas City did not come without challenges, but he faced these challenges and focused on serving his people with his hands-on governance style. His governance thrust is summarized in the acronym “CHANCES” which means Clean Up and Beautification, Health and Wellness, Agriculture and Aquaculture Support, Negosyo at Trabaho (Business and Employment), Community and Infrastructure Development, Education and Environment and Social Services.
